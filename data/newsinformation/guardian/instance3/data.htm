Christopher Samba's Champions League dream gives Arsenal hope

• Blackburn defender wants to play at highest level
• Arsenal target signed five-year contract last season

    • Tweet this
    • reddit this
  • Dominic Fifield in Hong Kong
  • guardian.co.uk, Wednesday 27 July 2011 22.30 BST
Christopher Samba says he wants Champions League football. Photograph: Martin Rickett/PA

The much-coveted Blackburn Rovers defender Christopher Samba has reiterated his desire to play in the Champions League and, in doing so, offered Arsenal further encouragement to pursue his signing this summer.

Samba, who captained Rovers in their 1-0 defeat to Aston Villa in the Barclays Asia Trophy hereon Wednesday, has indicated an eagerness to leave Ewood Park despite signing a new five-year contract at the club last season. The Congolese centre-half, 27, has attracted interest from Arsenal and Tottenham Hotspur and, while no bids have been received for the player, the Blackburn hierarchy are braced for unwelcome offers over the final month of the transfer window.

"I don't know what will happen, but at the minute I'm at Blackburn," Samba said. "We'll see what happens. If you get a player who has no ambition to play higher then he made a mistake and chose the wrong sport. He should do something else. When you're a footballer or play any high-profile sport, you should be ambitious. It's difficult at the moment, but I'm just waiting to see what happens and concentrating on my preparation.

"Of course I'd like to play in the Champions League. Which player doesn't dream of that? It would be a lie if I told you: 'Everything is good and I don't want to play in the Champions League.' That would be a big lie. Every player wants to do that. But I don't know about Arsenal as I'm not dealing with it. It's not my problem and I'm just trying to get myself fit."

Samba's ambition to compete in Europe's elite competition is shared by his manager at Blackburn, Steve Kean, who suggested earlier this week that it would be realistic for the club to aspire to qualification for the Champions League in "three or four years". To that end, the squad is awaiting the arrival of up to four players this summer, with the owners Balaji and Venkatesh Rao present at the Hong Kong stadium for the game with Villa ahead of talks with the management staff.

"Both brothers were here and we'll see them at the hotel and have a chat," added Kean, who hopes to add the Serbia midfielder Radosav Petrovic and Monaco's Cédric Mongongu to his ranks next week before turning his attentions to securing a forward. "We're trying to get a striker in, that's no secret, and we need bodies down the middle of the pitch where we've lost Phil Jones and Jermaine Jones. So we'll be trying hard before the start of the season."
