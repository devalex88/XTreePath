Aston Villa's Darren Bent moves on from England miss

• Striker scores three goals in two pre-season games
• Charles N'Zogbia set to be Villa's final summer signing

    • Tweet this
    • reddit this
  • Dominic Fifield in Hong Kong
  • guardian.co.uk, Wednesday 27 July 2011 22.30 BST
Darren Bent, second left, scores the only goal for Aston Villa in their defeat of Blackburn Rovers in the Barclays Asia Trophy in Hong Kong. Photograph: John Sibley/Action Images

Darren Bent has banished the memory of his glaring miss in last month's Euro 2012 qualifier against Switzerland and believes he can retain his place in Fabio Capello's England side through sheer weight of goals at Aston Villa before the finals next year.

The Aston Villa striker has had all summer to stew over the fluffed opportunity at Wembley which, if converted, would have helped England overcome a two-goal deficit to lead that match 3-2. Bent, a £24m signing from Sunderland in January, has scored three times in two pre-season fixtures, including the only goal in Wednesday's Barclays Asia Trophy victory over Blackburn Rovers, to suggest his form is restored.

"I tried to forget about the chance [against Switzerland]," said Bent, who stretched to apply the faintest touch to Barry Bannan's cross for Villa's winner in Hong Kong. "It happens to the best of people. It bothered me at the time, but you've got to move on from it and I've not mentioned it since.

"Some strikers miss chances and no one says a word afterwards. But, with me, I miss a chance and people just keep going on about it. I've just tried to get on with things. Over the years I've had to get stronger. I've had a few setbacks in my career, but I'm still here fighting."

The national team play Holland ahead of the new Premier League season next month, with Bent hopeful of keeping his starting place for the prestige fixture at Wembley. "To be England's No9 is fantastic, but there are still a lot of contenders out there like Andy Carroll, Jermain Defoe and others," he said. "So it's important for me to keep my form up. First and foremost, I want to do well for Villa, but it's a big incentive for me to have a major tournament at the end of the season and, hopefully, if I keep my form up I'll be there."

Bent has lost the supply line from which he benefited in the second half of last season following the departures of Ashley Young and Stewart Downing to Manchester United and Liverpool respectively. Yet those losses will be counterbalanced by the signing of Charles N'Zogbia from Wigan Athletic, and the continued development of Marc Albrighton on the opposite flank. "It's been a bit crazy and we've lost two fantastic players, but we showed against Blackburn that we can beat Premier League teams," Bent said. "If N'Zogbia comes, that will be a fantastic signing for us. Our aim is to finish above ninth and have a much more solid finish to the season."

N'Zogbia should complete his medical at Villa on Monday and be integrated into the squad next week though Alex McLeish – whose other significant purchase, Shay Given, excelled against Blackburn – does not envisage more signings unless players can be moved on. Any interest in West Ham's Scott Parker has apparently waned, with the management anticipating that their squad for the season ahead will be complete following N'Zogbia's £9.5m purchase.

"You can never say never but, if we get N'Zogbia which is looking good, it's unlikely we will do anything more unless there's any trading from the squad we already have," McLeish said. "We don't plan to. I believe we have some great young players, and I also believe that there are one or two 'sleepers' in there who could emerge. I know Ashley Young's boots are big ones to fill, but one or two could fill them.

"We're watching that the wages don't spiral out of control. People think free transfers don't cost you anything, but they may be on £50,000-£60,000 a week in wages. I know [the owner] Randy Lerner wants to keep it tighter and work within our means. That's normal, especially with financial fair play coming up in 2013. A lot of clubs will follow suit."
