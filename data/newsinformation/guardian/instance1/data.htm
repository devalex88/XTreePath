Essex slump to 144 all out at Southend as Leicestershire take control

Essex 144; Leicestershire 100-3

    • Tweet this
    • reddit this
  • George Dobell at Southend
  • guardian.co.uk, Wednesday 27 July 2011 22.25 BST
Ravi Bopara was one of the few Essex players to show much resistance. Photograph: Matt Bright

The word "festival" conjures up various images of jollity and fun. But it perhaps says everything about the Southend cricket festival that the Samaritans have pitched a tent next to the grandstand at Garon Park. This is a pragmatic rather than celebratory week of cricket. Designed generally to appease Essex members in the south-east of the county, it offers little of the traditional beauty that may be found at, say, Cheltenham.

Still, it brings the county game to an audience that may well be denied it otherwise – surely a worthy endeavour – while several Essex batsmen may be grateful for the opportunity to pop in for a chat with the Samaritans. Certainly the home side's performance on the first day against Leicestershire provided little reason for cheer.

Put in on a pitch that was slightly damp, Essex showed a lack of application in losing their final nine wickets for 87 runs as they crashed to 144 all out.

Only Ravi Bopara, who top-scored with 37, could count himself unfortunate. After battling for two-and-a-quarter hours he was trapped in front by a ball that kept impossibly low. All his colleagues, to a greater or lesser degree, played a hand in their own downfall. The England and Wales Cricket Board's pitch liaison officer, John Jameson, confirmed there was nothing untoward in the surface.

Nathan Buck, who took two wickets by maintaining a tight line and nipping the ball around off the seam, just shaded Wayne White – who also had two victims – as the pick of the Leicestershire seamers. Claude Henderson, gaining a little more turn than he would have expected on a first-day pitch, harnessed the conditions expertly to claim his best figures in a year – four for 43.

In what was left of the day Leicestershire made decent headway with the bat, reaching 100 for the loss of three wickets and suggesting they may be ready to make more of any available festival atmosphere than their hosts.
