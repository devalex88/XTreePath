Overview

Excalibur Hotel Casino is one of Las Vegas' top family-friendly and affordable properties, and its south Strip location provides easy access to all the city has to offer. Built inside a fanciful castle, Excalibur has an impressive medieval feel, with moats, suits of armor, and even some real-life jousting. Mix in modern revelry and restaurants, and you've got one unique Las Vegas experience.

Cheer on your knight over a fingers-only feast at the Tournament of Kings, Excalibur's popular dinner theater and signature attraction. (The hotel offers plenty of knife-and-fork dining options too, from the Steakhouse at Camelot to the more casual Roundtable Buffet.) Or travel back to the '70s at the Australian Bee Gees Show, where performers keep the original band's popular disco tunes alive in a multimedia performance. Another group of Australian stars, Thunder from Down Under, performs award-winning male strip shows for adults 18 and over.

After these shows, you may need a cooldown. Make a splash in Excalibur's all-ages pool, or chill out in style in a cabana at the 18-and-over pool. For some potentially money-making recreation, the casino and sports book offer everything you'd expect from a Vegas casino. If you didn't hit it big, you can still be pampered like a king or queen with a body wrap or facial at the Royal Treatment Spa. The spa's fitness center is reserved for guests 18 and older, but conveniently overlooks the pool area so you can keep an eye on the kids. Prefer a leisurely walk to a full-on workout? Take a stroll through Castle Walk, a medieval bazaar offering everything from Excalibur keepsakes to fudge-dipped apples. (Accessing other Strip shops, spas, and restaurants is made easy with a free tram connecting the Excalibur, Luxor, and Mandalay Bay--and an overhead pedestrian bridge leads to New York New York.)

When you retire to your private quarters, you'll find a room with medieval style and modern amenities, like widescreen TVs with premium cable channels, and high-speed wired Internet access (surcharge). Housed in two 28-story towers, many Excalibur rooms offer great views of The Strip, including skyline vistas of nearby New York New York Hotel & Casino.

Spa information

Spa options at the Royal Treatment Spa are tailored to fit most budgets and timetables. For a daily fee, you can take as little or as much time as you need in the sauna, steam room, and spa tub. If you've only got an hour or less, there are a host of treatments to choose from, including the Aromatherapeutic Sea Salt Glow facial or deep-tissue sports massage. Take half an afternoon--or half a day--and enjoy one of the many packaged options, like the Elements of Bliss package, which pairs a hot-stone massage with an aloe body wrap.

The Excalibur fitness center (limited to guests ages 18 and over) is located inside the spa, and access is included in the resort fee. With the pool overlook, you can keep an eye on the kids while getting in your workout routine.

Dining

Feast like royalty, or joke around like jesters at one of the onsite restaurants. Most of Excalibur's dining is casual and family-friendly. You can eat poolside at Drenched, or grab a Starbucks coffee and Krispy Kreme doughnut from the 24-hour Village Food Court. Room service is available 24 hours. Among the offerings:

The Steakhouse at Camelot - Excalibur's premiere dining option, this traditional steak and seafood restaurant features a cigar and wine room and a piano lounge. Watch the hotel's top chefs prepare dishes in the glassed-in show kitchen. Open for dinner only.

Dick's Last Resort - The surly staff at Dick's serves up messy food with attitude. Don't be afraid to dish it back. Watch the big game or enjoy some live music with a Ride 'Em Cowboy Rib Eye and one of 9 flavored daiquiris. If you're crowned with one of Dick's signature paper dunce hats, you'll know why they call this restaurant the "Shame o' The Strip."

Roundtable Buffet - With breakfast, lunch, and dinner buffets, there's something here for everyone. Enjoy traditional American favorites, as well as Chinese, Italian, and other international cuisine. All day, all-you-can-eat passes are available.

Sherwood Café - Burgers, pancakes, salads, and other classic American fare is served up from early morning to late night at this casual eatery.

Baja Fresh - The fresh salsa bar at this Mexican grill deserves mucho props. The health- conscious menu includes burritos, nachos, and quesadillas, and the restaurant offers a kids' menu.

Things to do

The medieval-style Tournament of Kings dinner show (no dining utensils) includes jousting, horseback-riding skill demonstrations, hand-to-hand combat, beasts, fire, and pageantry.

Six or seven nights weekly, Australia's all-male strip revue Thunder from Down Under performs in its own theater. (Guests must be 18 or older to attend. Those 18–20 must be accompanied by an adult.)

The Australian Bee Gees Show, A Tribute to the Bee Gees, has become the world's leading Bee Gees show. The group’s unbelievable resemblance and sound of the legendary Bee Gees has garnered international kudos from media and showgoers alike. The 75-minute multi-media concert event is presented from a contemporary perspective with state-of-the-art sound, superb lighting, and giant screens featuring video clips, live camera images, and stunning graphics.

Fantasy Faire Midway is an arcade of video and carnival games and includes two motion-simulator rides.

The 100,000-square-foot casino includes a poker room and a race-and-sports book.

Recreation

During those Las Vegas scorchers, head to Excalibur's outdoor pool facility. Chill out with an ice-cold cocktail from Drenched by the adults-only pool, or rent a cabana outfitted with a 42-inch plasma TV and refrigerator stocked with juice and water. A massive rock garden waterfall separates the all-ages pool and 2 water slides. The heated pools, 25-person spa tub, and firepits make this a relaxing evening destination.

A short guide to The Strip

Glittering, larger-than-life gambling palaces dominate Las Vegas Boulevard South, better known as The Strip. Here, hotels are destinations unto themselves. Within a three-mile stretch, visitors can see the New York City skyline, the Eiffel Tower, an Egyptian pyramid, imperial Rome, a dancing fountain, and an exploding volcano. A monorail links several hotels and the Las Vegas Convention Center.
